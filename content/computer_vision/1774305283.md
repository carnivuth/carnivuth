This detector relies on difference of Gaussian (DOG) in order to find keypoints

$$
DoG(x,y,\sigma) = (G(x,y,k\sigma) - G(x,y,\sigma))\ast I(x,y) = L(x,y,k\sigma) -L(x,y,\sigma)
$$

This approach is more computational efficient of computing [LOG](1774305314.md), and it's a good approximation of the results

![](/1777217954.png)

## Dog computing

Computation of dog is done by down-sampling and [gaussian smoothing](1774305286.md) the input image in order to obtain the scale space and then by computing differences between adjacent scale levels

![](/1777217955.png)

The next scale level is computed by taking half of the columns and rows and computing the same filter (performance optimization)

a point is detected as a feature if it's DoG is an extreme of its 26 neighbors

![](/1777217956.png)

## Dog improvements with filters

In order to localize keypoints in an accurate way and remove unstable point [filter procedures](1774305292.md) are needed
